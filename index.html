TOF → SNAP Centerline Converter

An automated pipeline for converting TOF-derived intracranial artery centerlines into SNAP-aligned centerlines using landmark-based affine registration.

Desktop Application

This Python-based desktop application converts Time-of-Flight (TOF) artery centerlines into SNAP-aligned centerlines using shared vascular landmarks.

  • • Input TOF centerlines (YAML)
  • • Input TOF & SNAP landmark files
  • • Computes affine transformation
  • • Outputs SNAP-compatible centerlines

Research Context

Multicontrast MRA studies often require arterial centerlines across imaging modalities. TOF provides high spatial resolution, while SNAP highlights vessel wall characteristics but frequently omits distal vessels. Manual reconstruction across contrasts can exceed three hours per scan.

Motivation

Reduce manual labor by registering a single TOF-derived centerline into SNAP space.

Approach

Affine transformation using five vascular landmarks: BA, A1_L, A1_R, M1_L, M1_R.

Impact

Replicates manual modeling accuracy in under 5 minutes, enabling complete SNAP centerlines.

Results

The affine registration achieves strong spatial agreement in central and proximal arteries, preserving topology and morphology. Minor deviations appear in highly tortuous distal vessels, but overall continuity remains intact. Processing time is reduced from ~3 hours to under 5 minutes per subject.
